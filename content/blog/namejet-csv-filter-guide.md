The secondary domain market is incredibly lucrative, but finding the needles in the haystack is a daily struggle for even the most experienced domain investors. Platforms like **NameJet**, DropCatch, and SnapNames release lists of thousands of expiring and deleting domains every single day. 

If you attempt to process these lists manually in Excel or Google Sheets, you are losing valuable time and potentially missing hidden gems. That's why building a systematic filtering process is critical.

### The Problem with Raw CSV Lists
When you download the raw CSV from a platform like NameJet, you are confronted with a chaotic mix of:
- Premium brandable domains
- Trademark-infringing domains
- Long, completely worthless hyphenated names
- Obscure TLDs (.net, .info, .biz)

You need a way to slice through this noise fast.

### The CVCV Pattern Strategy
One highly effective strategy is filtering for specific linguistic patterns. "CVCV" stands for **Consonant-Vowel-Consonant-Vowel** (for example, "Nomi" or "Viba"). 

These four-letter .coms are extremely valuable because they are short, highly pronounceable, and create perfect, memorable brands for software startups. Without a dedicated tool, isolating these patterns in a 10,000-row spreadsheet requires complex regex formulas.

### Using the CrushDomains Filter
We built the **CrushDomains Deleting Domains Filter** specifically to solve this workflow bottleneck. Here's a quick guide to supercharging your daily routine:

1. **Download your CSV** directly from NameJet's daily list.
2. **Upload it securely** into to the CrushDomains dashboard (all processing stays local to your browser for privacy).

By turning a 2-hour spreadsheet chore into a 30-second filter, you can focus purely on appraising the best domains and placing winning bids.
