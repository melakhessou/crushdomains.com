Domain investing is a nuanced market. Unlike traditional real estate, there are no neighborhood comps publicly available for every transaction. Many of the highest-value domain sales are protected by Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs).

This level of opacity makes **domain appraisals** incredibly difficult. How do you value an asset with no recurring revenue attached to it?

### The Two Values of Every Domain
When analyzing a domain's worth, professional domain investors typically look at two entirely separate numbers:

1. **Liquidity (Wholesale) Value:** This is the price you could reasonably expect another domain investor to pay for the asset at auction right now. It is driven purely by current market supply and demand metrics, rather than end-user potential.
2. **Retail (End-User) Value:** This is the price a funded startup, a local business, or an established corporation would pay because the domain is the perfect fit for their brand.

Most *automated domain appraisal tools* provide the retail value, leading to unrealistic expectations for amateur investors who attempt to flip a name overnight.

### Key Factors in AI Domain Valuation
Modern automated appraisers, like the **CrushDomains Instant Appraisal Tool**, rely on an amalgamation of core metrics:

- **Top-Level Domain (TLD) Authority:** The `.com` extension is the unquestionable king. An exact match `.com` might be worth $50,000, while the `.net` is worth $500, and the `.io` is worth $2,500 based purely on historical tech startup trends.
- **Length Constraint:** Shorter is inherently more valuable. Any pronounceable four-letter (LLLL) `.com` domain has intrinsic wholesale value because the supply is permanently capped.
- **Search Volume:** If a domain perfectly matches a high-volume commercial search term (e.g., `CarInsurance.com`), its value skyrockets due to direct-navigation traffic.
- **Brandability over Keywords:** Exact-match domains (EMDs) dominated the early 2000s. Today, smooth, brandable, highly-pronounceable, made-up words hold massive sway over tech companies looking for trademarkable assets.

### How to Use Appraisals Wisely
You should never view an automated domain appraisal as a guaranteed price tag. It is a benchmark. 

If you are buying domains, run a bulk list through an appraisal engine to filter out completely worthless names. Use the estimates as a compass to guide your negotiation. If you own a premium domain, the best strategy remains patience: the right buyer will eventually find it, and they will pay retail.
